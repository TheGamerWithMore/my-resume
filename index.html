GovHack 2016 - 2017

GovHack is the open data hacking competition sponsored by the South Australian state government. In GovHack, we had access to the entire South Australian state government's open data website Data.SA , we used this website to obtain data about how many library books get borrowed, how often they get borrowed and where to find them in libraries accross South Australia. In 2016, my team LibHackers achieved an honorable mention for our project Library Book Finder. We all had limited skill at the time with CSS and HTML. In 2017, we had our first and only victory with our project R_U_B_B_I_S_H, which was a PoC (Proof of Concept) application for Android and iOS. The app could scan special R_U_B_B_I_S_H QR (quick response) codes to determine what items could be thrown away in the recycle bin or compost bin. It could also alert the user when their bin schedule is, so (for example) the elderly don't forget to put the bin out. The app was also going to have text to speech functionalities for vision impared persons to understand what can and can't be thrown away. Including big text to help the vision impared understand what can and can't be thrown away.
Our team was going to implement the Google Speech API (TalkBack/Text to Speech) to help said vision impared persons. Please have a peruse at the PoC video.
